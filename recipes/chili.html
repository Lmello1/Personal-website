The following is a simple chili recipe that was a staple during college meal prep. Simple to make, and super cheep cal/$ ratio! And it tastes 1000 times better than the stuff from a can. Feel free to tweak anything in this recipe, as it is the spice level is very mild.
Optional items are marked with *, replacement options are marked with ()
Chili

  • yellow onion
  • garlic
  • ground beef
  • kidney beans *
  • black beans (any canned beans)
  • diced tomato
  • beef stock (water)
  • tomato paste *

Chili seasoning mix

  • chili powder
  • brown sugar
  • ground cumin
  • salt
  • onion powder
  • black pepper
  • garlic powder
  • cayenne powder

With a ratio of
1:1 chili powder | 1 tbsp
2:1 brown sugar | 1/2 tbsp
3:1 ground cumin | 1 tsp
3:1 salt | 1 tsp
6:1 onion powder | 1/2 tsp
6:1 black pepper | 1/2 tsp
12:1 garlic powder | 1/4 tbs
12:1 cayenne powder | 1/4 tbs
Example measurements for 1 tbsp of chili seasoning is given above as well

Start with onions and garlic, cook with oil until onion is soft.

Add ground beef and get a good brown on it, continue until almost fully cooked

Drain and add beans, tomato, tomato paste, beef stock, and chili seasoning mix.

Keep on low heat for 30-60 minutes
